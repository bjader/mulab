What does muLab actually do?

Currently, the muLab software provides realistic data simulation for the following undergraduate experiments:

Low temperature calorimetry

Calculating the low temperature thermal properties of copper using liquid nitrogen cryogens.

Muon lifetime

Calculating the mean lifetime of atmospheric muons detected by a plastic based scintillator.

For each of these experiments a mixture of real-life data and experimental theory has been used to produce a graphical result similar to what the student should expect to see in the laboratory. From here students can adjust parameters and instantly observe the effect this might have on their final results. This responsiveness and live feedback is a huge improvement on real life, whereby it can take up to 24 hours for students to see how changes impacted their results.
